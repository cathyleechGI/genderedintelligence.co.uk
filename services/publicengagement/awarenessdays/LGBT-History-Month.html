--- title: LGBT History Month ---

370 BC

In ancient Greece, Plato writes the Symposium...

In it he proposes a theory of love and identity that accounts for a range of sexualities and gender identities – and which don’t map neatly onto our modern understandings!. Often the perception is that LGBT+ acceptance is a relatively recent phenomenon, but there is plenty of evidence that this is not the case. Sexual and gender diversity are present throughout recorded history, and was often much more widely accepted than the present day.

1533

The Buggery Act outlaws male homosexuality in England for the first time.

1753

The first statutory legislation in England and Wales, the Clandestine Marriages Act, is introduced.

Until this point marriages had been governed by canon law, and there were few legal requirements. Although canon law did not permit equal marriage, we can speculate that prior to the introduction of legal restrictions it would have been possible for a person ‘living as a man/woman’ to get married with comparative ease. This Act marks a turning point from which who can get married - and how - is increasingly regulated and monitored by the state.
